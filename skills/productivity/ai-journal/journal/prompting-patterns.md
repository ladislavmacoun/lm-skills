# Adam's Prompting Patterns

*How Adam communicates with AI - patterns, style, and effectiveness.*

## Input Method

Adam primarily uses **voice-to-text** for prompting. This results in:
- Phonetic spelling errors throughout ("teh" for "the", "obsiidna" for "obsidian")
- Run-on sentences that flow like speech
- Natural, conversational tone rather than structured prompts
- Ideas that build and evolve within a single prompt

## Prompting Style

### Strengths
- **High-level vision is always clear** despite typos - intent comes through strongly
- **Provides context about constraints** (e.g., "MCP server seems to work", "it cannot update files outside the repo")
- **Thinks out loud** - shares reasoning, not just instructions
- **Comfortable with ambiguity** - gives direction and trusts AI to fill gaps

### Areas for Growth
- Could benefit from breaking long voice prompts into shorter, focused messages
- Sometimes combines multiple decisions in one prompt (storage location + file structure + trigger behavior) - separating these could lead to better outcomes
